ğŸ”¬ The Science Behind It
Single-Slit Diffraction
When light passes through a single narrow opening, it doesn't just go straight through - it spreads out and creates a pattern with:

A bright center spot
Dimmer spots on either side
Dark areas in between

Double-Slit Diffraction
When light passes through two narrow openings close together:

The light waves from both slits overlap and interfere
This creates many bright and dark bands (called fringes)
The pattern proves light behaves like waves
This is one of the most important experiments in quantum physics!

â“ Common Questions
Why does the pattern spread out more with smaller slits?
Smaller openings cause more wave spreading (diffraction). It's like water waves going through a narrow gap.
What makes the dark spots in double-slit?
The waves from both slits cancel each other out at certain angles - this is called destructive interference.
Why are the numbers so small?
Light waves are incredibly tiny! We use scientific notation: 5e-7 means 0.0000005 meters.
Can I see this in real life?
Yes! You can do this experiment with a laser pointer and thin slits cut in aluminum foil.
